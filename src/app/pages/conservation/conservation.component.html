Background

Turtle races, unfortunately, have a very checkered history when it comes to animal welfare and turtle conservation. As early as 1925 – a year after turtle races first appeared in Oklahoma – a concerted effort was launched by Kansas citizens to outlaw the practice, on the grounds that it was cruel to animals and depleting box turtle populations. Subsequent investigations in 1968, 1977, 1997 and 2005 have all found major problems with turtle races. Four states (Maryland, Virginia, California and Florida) have now banned or significantly limited turtle races.

The Turtle Race Task Force (TRTF) was created in 2019 with the goal of convincing state wildlife agencies to ban turtle races. While our investigation found major problems with most races, a few races that were well done made an impression on us. We learned that not only are turtle races important to the communities that have them, but that at their best turtle races can be humane and serve as a vehicle for conservation.

To aide turtle race organizers in changing their races, the TRTF is currently drafting guidelines for an "ideal" turtle race. We expect to release these by Spring 2022. Races that conform to the guidelines will receive the TRTF's Humane and Sustainable certification, which may be displayed at the race and used in advertising to indicate that race staff have gone above and beyond to make their event turtle friendly.

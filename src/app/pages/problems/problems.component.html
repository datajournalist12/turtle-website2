Why Turtle Conservationists are Concerned about Turtle Racing

Turtle races are events that are common at fairs and festivals in some areas of the United States. While the concept may sound innocent, thousands of wild turtles are collected for the practice every year by race participants. The Turtle Race Task Force (TRTF) has mapped out over 600 festivals with annual turtle races.

Unlike other wildlife related activities like fishing and hunting, turtle races have not developed a code of ethics – a "Conservation Ethic" – that prevents harm to individual turtles and damage to turtle populations. The events are failing to turn children toward wildlife conservation, and most turtle race organizers make no effort to contribute towards the conservation of the species they use.
